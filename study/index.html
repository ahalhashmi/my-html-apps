🧲 Magnets

Key rules

Opposite poles attract: North + South pull together.
Same poles repel: North + North or South + South push away.
Broken magnet: when a magnet breaks, each piece becomes a new magnet with its own North and South pole.

⚡ Electricity

Conductors and insulators

Conductor

A material that readily allows electric charges to pass through.

Examples: metals, copper, gold, silver, iron, steel.

Insulator

A material that resists the flow of electric charges.

Examples: glass, wood, plastic, oil, diamond, paper, rubber, air, fabric, cotton.

Plug safety idea

The parts you hold and the wire covering are insulators. The metal parts that go into the outlet are good conductors.

💡 Circuits

Series and parallel circuits

Diagram 1: Series

  • One pathway for current.
  • Adding a second bulb makes the first bulb less bright.
  • If one part breaks, the circuit can stop.

Diagram 2: Parallel

  • Two or more pathways for current.
  • Bulb brightness stays the same when another bulb is connected.
  • One device can work without affecting the others.

🌈 Light

Transparent, translucent, opaque

Transparent: light passes completely through the object.
Translucent: light passes partially through the object.
Opaque: light does not pass through the object.
Remember: transparent = clear, translucent = partly clear, opaque = blocks light.

Reflection

Exam answer: Reflection of light occurs when light bounces back off a surface, changing its direction.

Refraction

Exam answer: Refraction is the bending of light rays as they pass from one medium to another, changing the path of the rays. It happens because the speed of the light ray or wave changes.
